Metal roofing has grown rapidly in popularity across Utah over the past decade — and for good reason. But it's also a bigger upfront investment than traditional asphalt shingles. So is it actually worth it for Utah homeowners?

## What Does Metal Roofing Cost in Utah?

In Utah, metal roofing typically costs between $10,000 and $20,000 for a standard residential home, depending on roof size, pitch, and the type of metal used. That's roughly 2–3x the cost of a quality asphalt shingle roof.

## How Long Does Metal Roofing Last?

A quality metal roof installed by a licensed contractor can last 40 to 70 years. Compared to asphalt shingles at 20–25 years, you may never need to replace your roof again.

## How Does Metal Hold Up in Utah's Climate?

Very well — arguably better than any other material:
- **Snow:** Metal's slick surface sheds snow instead of holding it, reducing structural load
- **Hail:** Metal roofs are highly impact-resistant and often rated Class 4 (the highest rating)
- **UV:** Metal doesn't degrade from sun exposure the way asphalt does
- **Wind:** Properly installed metal roofs can withstand high winds common on the Wasatch Front

## Energy Savings

Metal roofs reflect solar heat rather than absorbing it. Utah homeowners commonly report lower air conditioning costs in summer after switching to metal.

## Insurance Benefits

Many Utah homeowners insurance providers offer discounts for metal roofs due to their superior durability. The discount may offset some of the upfront cost difference over time.

## The Verdict

If you're planning to stay in your home long-term and want a roof you'll never have to think about again, metal is worth the investment. If you're planning to sell within 5–10 years, asphalt shingles are more cost-effective.
