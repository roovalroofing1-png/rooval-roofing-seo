If you've lived in Utah for more than a few years, you've seen what a hailstorm can do to a neighborhood. What most homeowners don't know is that hail damage is often fully covered by homeowners insurance — but only if you act quickly and work with the right people.

## Does Homeowners Insurance Cover Hail Damage?

In most cases, yes. Standard homeowners insurance policies cover sudden damage from hail and wind. The key word is "sudden" — insurers will deny claims for damage they consider gradual or due to lack of maintenance.

## How Long Do You Have to File?

Most Utah insurance policies require you to file a claim within 1 year of the damage event, though some allow up to 2 years. Don't assume you have unlimited time — file promptly after any significant storm.

## Step 1: Get a Professional Inspection First

Before you call your insurance company, have a roofing contractor inspect your roof. This documents the damage professionally and tells you whether a claim is worth filing.

## Step 2: File Your Claim

Call your insurance company's claims line and report the damage. They'll assign an adjuster who will schedule a visit to inspect your roof.

## Step 3: Have Your Contractor Present at the Adjuster Visit

This is the step most homeowners skip. Insurance adjusters work for the insurance company, not for you. Having your roofing contractor present ensures all damage is documented and nothing gets missed.

## Step 4: Review the Estimate Carefully

The insurance company will send you an estimate of covered costs. Review it with your contractor before accepting. Sometimes items are left off or undervalued.
