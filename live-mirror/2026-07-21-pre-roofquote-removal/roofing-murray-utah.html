Roof Work Done Right in Murray, Utah

Murray sits near the center of Salt Lake County, where the ground slopes gently west from the Wasatch foothills down toward the Jordan River. It is an old smelter town that grew into a settled valley-floor community, and you can read that history in its rooflines — turn-of-the-century cottages near the Murray Downtown Historic District, postwar Cape Cods, and newer infill homes all sharing the same streets. Landmarks like Wheeler Historic Farm and the tree-shaded Murray City Park give the city its character, and a lot of the homes around them are carrying roofs that have quietly aged past their prime. Rooval Roofing is a local, family-run crew that keeps those roofs sound, one honest job at a time.

The Kind of Roofing Help Murray Homes Need

Whether you own a 1950s cottage off Vine Street or a two-story build closer to Fashion Place, we handle the full range of residential roofing. Every job starts with a real look at your roof and a straight answer about what it actually needs — not what pads an invoice.

  • Roof repair — leaks, wind-lifted shingles, flashing failures, and worn valleys, tracked down to the source and fixed so the water stays outside where it belongs.
  • Roof replacement — complete tear-offs and new installs in asphalt, architectural shingle, or standing-seam, built to hold up to Utah’s freeze-and-thaw swings.
  • Metal roofing — a long-lived option that sheds snow cleanly and shrugs off the intense summer sun, and a favorite for Murray homeowners who would rather not re-roof again in a decade.
  • Roof tune-up — a focused maintenance visit to reseal exposed nails, refresh worn flashing, and clear the small problems before they turn into a ceiling stain.
  • Gutters — proper drainage that carries meltwater away from your fascia, foundation, and basement instead of letting it pool where it does damage.

Storms roll through the valley every season. When one passes over Murray, we will come out, inspect your roof, and document any storm damage with clear photos so you have a record in hand. What you choose to do with that record is entirely up to you — we are here to tell you the truth about your roof, nothing more.

What Murray Roofs Are Up Against

Murray’s weather is easier on roofs than the high benches, but it has its own wear pattern, and knowing it is half of doing the job well.

Canyon-mouth wind. The city catches the gusts that spill out of the Wasatch canyons to the east, and those bursts can reach damaging speeds without much warning. They tend to find the weak spots first — lifting the edges of older three-tab shingles and peeling back flashing that has already gone brittle.

Freeze-thaw and ice dams. Valley-floor snow does not always melt in an orderly way. On the low-slope sections and complex rooflines common in Murray’s older neighborhoods, meltwater refreezes at the eaves, backs up under the shingles, and works its way into the deck. Sound underlayment and proper attic ventilation are what keep that cycle from reaching your ceiling.

High-UV summer aging. At better than four thousand feet, the summer sun is punishing. It dries out asphalt shingles, curls their edges, and quietly bakes years off a roof’s life — which is why so many Murray roofs give out earlier than their original label promised.

Housing age. A large share of Murray’s homes went up between the smelter years and the postwar boom, and many still wear roofs installed over builder-grade decking. Mature trees drop debris into the gutters, and the seams and penetrations on these older roofs need a closer eye. We check the wood underneath, not just the surface, and tell you exactly what we find.

Murray Roofing Questions We Hear Often

What does a new roof cost on a typical Murray home? Most Murray roof replacements land somewhere between $20,000 and $30,000, depending on size, pitch, and material. Older homes near the historic core sometimes need a little decking repair once the old layers come off — we check for it and quote it upfront so the number you approve is the number you pay.

My house is from the ASARCO smelter days — is it too old to re-roof properly? Not at all. Century-old Murray homes re-roof beautifully. The key is looking under the shingles at the deck and framing first, which is exactly what our inspection does, so any soft spots get addressed before the new roof goes down.

Do I really need to worry about ice dams this far down in the valley? On many homes, no. But low-slope additions, north-facing sections, and complex rooflines around Murray’s older streets do dam up, and the fix is usually about ventilation and underlayment more than the shingles themselves. We will tell you honestly whether yours is at risk.

How long will the work actually take? Most single-family Murray roofs are finished in one day. Larger or more intricate homes may run into a second day. Our crew shows up early, works clean, and does a full nail-and-debris sweep before we leave your driveway.

Frequently Asked Questions — Roofing in Murray, Utah

How much does a roof replacement cost in Murray, Utah?

Murray roof replacements typically run $20,000 to $30,000. Many Murray homes date from the mid-20th century and may need decking repairs as part of the project, something we check for and quote upfront.

How long does a roof replacement take?

Most single-family homes in Utah are completed in 1-2 days: tear-off and dry-in the first day, finish work the same or next day. Larger, steeper, or complex roofs can take longer. Our crew arrives early, works efficiently, and does a thorough cleanup before leaving.

What roofing materials work best for Utah's climate?

Utah's hot dry summers, cold winters, UV intensity, and occasional hail demand durable materials. Architectural asphalt shingles with a Class 3 or Class 4 impact rating offer the best value for most homeowners. Metal roofing sheds snow easily and can last 40-70 years. We help you choose the right material for your home and budget.

Does homeowner's insurance cover roof storm damage in Utah?

Most homeowner's insurance policies in Utah cover sudden storm damage including hail, wind, and falling trees. Age-related wear is not covered. Rooval Roofing inspects and documents any storm damage with dated photos and a written report at no cost - you keep the records and decide whether to involve your insurer.

How do I know if I need a repair or a full roof replacement?

Repairs work for isolated damage: a few missing shingles, minor flashing leaks, or limited granule loss. A full replacement makes more sense when your roof is over 20 years old, damage is widespread, or leaks keep recurring. We offer free inspections and always give you an honest recommendation.

Do you handle both repairs and full replacements in Murray?

Yes. Rooval Roofing covers everything from minor leak repairs and chimney reflashing to complete tear-off and replacement. We serve all Murray neighborhoods and can usually schedule an inspection within a few business days.
