## The two GEO "do more" gaps (from their own playbook)
1. **Off-site authority (their Step 7) = our WS7** — backlinks, digital PR (get quoted by KSL/local news using the NOAA storm data), directory listings, genuine presence in LLM-trusted communities (Reddit/YouTube). This is THE lever that makes GEO work: ranking + off-site authority drives AI citations. #1 priority.
2. **Pillar-cluster hubs (their Step 4)** — tie existing pages into deliberate topic hubs (e.g., "Utah Storm & Hail Roof Damage" pillar with storm/city pages clustering up). Strengthens topical authority. Medium effort.

## Broader growth levers from their full menu (fit Rooval's rules)
- Content cadence — monthly expert local guides (compounds)
- Deeper CRO — beyond the one inspection landing page
- AI chatbot (their GenAI division) — 24/7 site assistant for roof Q&A + lead capture (real idea, dev-heavy)
- Email / lead nurture — convert more of the leads already coming in
- Reviews engine — systematic review requests (owner-run)

## Deliberately SKIP (owner's organic-only rule)
Their entire paid-media division: PPC, Google Ads, YouTube Ads, LinkedIn Ads, Amazon Ads. Big part of their revenue; off-limits for Rooval.

## Bottom line
The clearest "do more" from BOTH our plan and WebSpero's own GEO methodology is the same: **WS7 — off-site authority.** It's the fuel that makes everything already built rank and get cited.
