H3: What happens to asphalt shingles during prolonged hot weather?
Explain in homeowner-friendly language:
* Asphalt materials absorb heat.
* Roof surfaces can become substantially hotter than the surrounding air.
* Repeated temperature changes may contribute to material expansion and contraction.
* UV exposure gradually affects asphalt and protective surfaces.
* Aging shingles may become less flexible over time.
* Exposed sealants and flashing materials may deteriorate for several reasons, including age and weather exposure.
H3: Does one heatwave ruin a roof?
Answer directly:
* Usually not, if the roof is properly installed, adequately ventilated and in good condition.
* A heatwave may expose or accelerate an existing problem.
* An older or compromised roof may respond differently from a recently installed roof.
* A professional inspection is needed to establish the actual cause of visible deterioration.

H3: What temperature is too hot for roof shingles?
Explain carefully:
* There is no single outdoor temperature at which every asphalt roof suddenly fails.
* Roof-surface temperature depends on color, orientation, ventilation, sunlight, slope and surrounding conditions.
* Manufacturer specifications and installation requirements matter more than a universal threshold.
* Avoid presenting an unsupported temperature as a guaranteed damage point.
This section should be written for answer extraction but should not invent an exact “danger temperature.”
H3: Can roof shingles melt in hot weather?
Provide a brief answer:
* Properly manufactured and installed asphalt shingles are designed for outdoor exposure.
* Normal summer weather should not cause them to melt like liquid asphalt.
* Softened sealants, surface scuffing or installation-related damage may be mistaken for melting.
* Visible deformation should be inspected rather than diagnosed from appearance alone.

H2: Can a Hot Attic Damage Roof Shingles?
Answer the fan-out query directly.
