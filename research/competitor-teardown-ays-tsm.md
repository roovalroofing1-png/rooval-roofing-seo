# Competitor SEO Teardown — AYS Roofing + TSM Roofs (2026-07-02)

## Summary

Rooval's foundation (city URL pattern, hub-and-spoke links, FAQPage with real cost ranges, genuine geography copy) is already sound, so the plan is depth and trust rather than new templates: fix the broken entity schema (stale 17-review count, 404 logo, duplicate nodes, no per-city scoping), then adopt TSM's two proven moves — a small, genuinely-unique city-x-service page tier and bidirectional blog-service-city interlinking — while thickening the 12 city pages toward 1,000+ words with named neighborhoods and Lehi-proximity copy. Conversion-side, borrow AYS's single-money-page funnel by consolidating all three conflicting CTA targets onto /roof-quote/, and borrow TSM's crawlable review text, on-page address-capture form, snippet-formatted blog template, and owner-as-person E-E-A-T touches. Nearly everything is executable by an agent with WP admin + REST access; explicitly avoid AYS's thin doorway pages and unproofed find-replace workflow, TSM's self-serving star-rating schema, and any insurance-claims or year-specific warranty language per the owner's hard constraints.

## Adopt List (ranked)

### 1. Fix and deepen the LocalBusiness entity graph into a single authoritative RoofingContractor node with per-city scoping
- **Source:** TSM (deep schema) + Rooval baseline (errors)
- **Effort:** hours | agent-executable: True
- **Evidence:** TSM ships one Rank Math @graph with a RoofingContractor node carrying full NAP, geo, hasMap, openingHours, a 24-service makesOffer catalog linking every service URL, areaServed for all 13 cities, license number, GAF/OC/BBB credentials as additionalProperty, and 6 sameAs profiles. Rooval's current schema has confirmed errors: aggregateRating reviewCount stale at 17 (site says 30+, actual is 31), logo URL /rooval-roofing-logo.webp returns 404, duplicate un-@id-linked RoofingContractor entities per page, Article schema + og:type=article on commercial pages, identical block on every city page (zero per-city areaServed/Service scoping), hub FAQ has no FAQPage markup, sameAs missing the Google Business Profile.
- **Rooval adaptation:** One pass across all 12 /roofing-{city}-utah/ pages, /service-areas/, and /metal-roofing-utah/: merge the hand-rolled block into the Rank Math @graph via @id so there is ONE RoofingContractor entity; update reviewCount to 31; point logo to the real /wp-content/uploads/2025/10/ file; add GBP + any Facebook/Nextdoor URLs to sameAs; add makesOffer entries with URLs for /metal-roofing-utah/, /roof-quote/, and each service; scope each city page with its own Service node (serviceType 'Roof repair/replacement', areaServed = that City-in-Utah only); add the missing FAQPage block to /service-areas/; swap Article→WebPage/ItemPage and og:type article→website on service/city pages. Do NOT add TSM's Product-with-aggregateRating star hack.

### 2. Two-tier local architecture: add city-x-service child pages under the existing city set for top service+city long-tails
- **Source:** TSM
- **Effort:** days | agent-executable: True
- **Evidence:** TSM runs 13 city hubs + ~65 programmatic city-x-service pages at /service-areas/{city}/{service}/ with 1,800-2,000 words of substantially unique copy each (only ~26% 8-word-shingle overlap between Tempe and Scottsdale same-service pages), each carrying its own 3-question FAQPage schema and a Step 1/2/3 how-it-works outline — programmatic local SEO that dodges doorway thinness. This is the teardown's own 'single most copyable move for Rooval.'
- **Rooval adaptation:** Start small and unique, not at AYS scale: for the 4-5 highest-value cities (Lehi, Provo, Orem, American Fork, Draper) create 2 child pages each — roof replacement and metal roofing (leveraging the new /metal-roofing-utah/ topical authority), e.g. /roofing-lehi-utah/roof-replacement/ or /roof-replacement-lehi-utah/ to match the existing slug convention. Each page: 1,200+ original words, a 3-step 'how it works in {city}' section, city-specific material angle (snow-load and ice-dam performance at bench elevations, high-UV asphalt aging at 4,500 ft), that city's cost range, 3 unique FAQs with FAQPage schema, links up to its city hub and across to the flagship service page. Storm-damage variants must say 'storm damage inspection & documentation' only — no claims-help framing. Expand to more cities only after these index and earn impressions.

### 3. Deepen the 12 city pages: more unique words, named neighborhoods on every page, Lehi-proximity copy, and rotated section wording

Rooval's baseline also flags no 'based in Lehi, X minutes away' proximity copy anywhere.

Rotate the H2 labels per city AYS-style ('Roofing Services for Draper Homes' vs 'What Orem Roofs Are Up Against') instead of one fixed skeleton, and vary the 4 currently word-for-word-identical FAQs per city. Also drop '| Rooval Roofing' from the H1s.

### 4. Bidirectional topic-cluster interlinking: blog↔service modules, city→service deep links, and fixed footer anchors

All original anchor phrasing, always https.

### 5. Single-money-page funnel: consolidate every CTA onto /roof-quote/

### 6. Crawlable review content: a /reviews/ page plus real testimonial text on city pages near the CTA

- **Effort:** hours | agent-executable: True

## Do NOT copy
- AYS's thin programmatic doorway city pages (368-570 unique words, ~45% repeated chrome, identical 5-section skeleton, paraphrased identical FAQs, plus an overlapping second '/residential-roofing-{city}/' page set that self-cannibalizes) — confirmed do-not-copy from the prior teardown; Google's scaled-content policies make this cluster fragile, and duplicating city intent across two page sets splits equity.
- AYS's unproofed find-replace/AI template workflow — the Kenwood page shipped with a raw editing instruction ('Live H1 fix: it currently reads "Kentwood"...') live in the rendered copy. Any templated generation for Rooval must go through a per-page QA diff (grep for bracket placeholders, wrong city names, editing notes) before publish.
- TSM's Product 'Roofing Services' node with aggregateRating 4.8/536 and self-attributed LocalBusiness aggregateRating — a star-snippet hack that violates Google's self-serving review-snippet guidelines; Rooval's own sitewide identical aggregateRating already carries this risk and should be scoped carefully (kept only where eligible), not amplified.
- AYS's keyword-stuffed Organization schema name ('Roofing Contractor in Cincinnati, Northern Kentucky & Cleveland | AYS' instead of the business name) — schema name must be the real legal/brand name; stuffing it risks GBP/entity mismatch.
- AYS's 'storm/hail + insurance-claim help' service positioning and anchors — hard constraint violation. Rooval only inspects and documents storm damage for the homeowner to file; never claims help, adjuster meetings, free-roof, or deductible language on any page, including new city-x-service storm pages.
- Any specific-year warranty claims like AYS's 'up to 25-year warranty' (which they themselves state inconsistently: 5yr vs 25yr across the site) — Rooval says 'workmanship warranty' only, and never '15-year warranty'.
- Client-side-only review widgets as the sole social proof (AYS's Elfsight strip) — zero crawlable review text; if a widget is used, pair it with server-rendered review quotes.
- TSM's protocol sloppiness — http:// internal links, sitemap and canonical URLs on http while homepage is https, and duplicate conflicting Organization @ids; when the agent generates links or schema for Rooval, everything must be absolute https and share one @id graph.
- TSM's title CTR bait copied verbatim ('FREE Quote!'/'FREE Estimates!' with exclamation caps) — Rooval's 5.0★ title device already occupies that slot; stacking shouty offer bait would look cloned and cost brand trust. Same for cloning their weak templated child-page metas ('Contact us to get a quote.').
- Page-weight bloat patterns: TSM's 780-990KB HTML with a 40-language translator and accessibility-widget DOM junk, AYS's nav duplicated 3x in the DOM and a live BugHerd QA script with API key in production — Rooval has active Lighthouse optimization work; add no sitewide third-party widgets without measuring.
