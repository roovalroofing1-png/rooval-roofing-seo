## The 60-second read

We are running **two engines at once**, and they run on **two different clocks**.

**Engine 1 — the Lead Engine.** Books roof inspections from the rest of the 2026 storm season. Works in *days*. Lives on Google Business Profile, Facebook/Instagram, and how fast we call a new lead back. Augustina owns most of it. **This is where the money is this year.**

**Engine 2 — the Ranking & Citation Engine.** Gets our website onto page 1 of Google, and eventually gets Rooval *named inside AI answers* (ChatGPT, Google AI, Perplexity). Builds slowly through the off-season, pays off into 2027. Wife-via-Claude-Code owns most of it.

We keep the two on **separate scoreboards** so a Facebook lead is never mistaken for an "AI win."

**The three highest-value moves, in order:**
1. **Turn on Google Business Profile + Facebook/Instagram for storm season** — fastest leads we have.
2. **Push the pages that already rank #9–20 onto page 1** — these already rank, so they can move in *weeks*.
3. **Ship the two storm pages + the Utah storm-documentation guide** — the exact page types AI likes to quote.

**The one rule that overrides everything — the insurance guardrail:**

> *"We inspect your roof and document storm damage for your records, so you can file with your insurer. We're roofers, not insurance agents — you and your insurer handle the claim."*

**Never** say: deductible, "free roof," "we file for you," "we meet your adjuster," or anything that explains insurance coverage. **Always fine:** "free roof inspection," "workmanship warranty" (never "15-year warranty"). Every post, ad, page, and interview gets a compliance check *before* it goes live. The phrase "so you can file" is the locked outer edge — nothing may drift it toward "we file for you." ("Give you what you need to file" is retired: it reads as a claim-ready-package promise, and we don't make one.)

**What we will NOT do:** no mass AI-written content, no fake author byline (the "Mike Sorensen" author box comes off the live site in days 1–2), no review gating, no fake Reddit accounts, no promises about *when* AI will cite us. Details in Section 4.

## 4. What we deliberately will NOT do — and why

1. **No mass or calendar-driven AI content.** The March 2026 Google update cut thin-AI sites 50–80%; the guardrail bans it; the old volume script stays retired.
2. **No schema built as a "citation trick."** The "2.8x citations" claim is refuted and FAQ rich results were dropped in 2023. We fix schema for hygiene only (WS5) and never report it as a GEO win.
3. **No llms.txt file.** No proven benefit.
4. **No citation-date promises.** Citations are a monitored trend. The committed 90-day items are the inputs we control — rewrites shipped, links built, pages published, cadence kept; rankings, clicks, and bookings are targets we track against, not guarantees.
5. **No claims-adjacent content, ever.** No deductible explainers, no "will insurance pay" posts, no filing-steps or deadline guides, no adjuster-meeting offers, no "free roof" hooks — even though competitors rank with them and the Evidence-Package seminar teaches them. Owner directive beats tactics. Every publish (including mined customer quotes and ad copy) passes the compliance check.
6. **No review gating.** No star-average target, no incentives, no screening, no suppression — every completed job gets a request, whatever the sentiment.
7. **No astroturfing.** No fake accounts, alt accounts, hidden affiliation, or vote manipulation — on Reddit, in Facebook community groups, in GBP Q&A, or anywhere.
8. **No A/B or multivariate testing platform.** Nothing reaches significance at ~15 clicks/28 days; we make bold changes and compare before/after.
9. **No UTMs for organic AI traffic, no HowTo schema, no retired tools** (Structured Data Testing Tool, Google Optimize stack, Trusted Stores, Like Box).
