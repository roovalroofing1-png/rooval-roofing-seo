What Lehi Weather Does to a Roof

Lehi sits on a bench between the valley floor and the mountains, and that position shapes almost everything about how a roof wears here. A few conditions come up again and again on our inspections:

  • Bench-elevation snow and ice dams. Homes higher up toward Traverse Mountain hold snow longer than homes down by the lake. When snow melts during the day and refreezes at the eaves overnight, ice dams form and back water up under the shingles. Proper attic ventilation and ice-and-water membrane at the eaves are what keep that meltwater outside where it belongs.
  • Canyon and Point-of-the-Mountain wind. Lehi catches gusts funneling out of the canyons and through the gap at the Point, and wind is patient. It lifts and loosens shingle edges over years, then peels them off in one hard storm. Ridge caps, hips, and any spot with older sealant are usually the first to let go.
  • High-desert UV. The same clear, sunny skies that make Lehi a good place to live are hard on asphalt shingles. Intense high-altitude sun bakes the oils out of the shingle mat, so granules shed and the surface gets brittle. South- and west-facing slopes almost always age faster than the rest of the roof.
  • Freeze-thaw cycling. Warm afternoons and hard-freezing nights are normal here through the shoulder seasons. Water works into tiny cracks, freezes, expands, and pries them wider. Over enough winters that cycle finds every weak seam and worn flashing on the roof.

Lehi's Building Boom Is Now Reaching Roof Age

Here is the part a lot of Lehi homeowners have not thought about. The city was a quiet farming town for most of its history, then the tech corridor along I-15 turned it into the heart of Silicon Slopes, and homes went up fast through the 2000s and 2010s. Big master-planned communities like Holbrook Farms and Ivory Ridge filled in during that stretch, along with much of Traverse Mountain.

Asphalt roofs do not last forever, and a lot of that builder-grade material is now old enough to be near the end of its service life. If your home went up during Lehi's growth years, the original roof may be quietly running out of runway even though the house still feels new. That is exactly the kind of roof we look at every week, and catching it before a leak starts saves you the water damage that comes after.
